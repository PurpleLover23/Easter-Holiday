All about Easter Eggs and The Easter Bunny!

Easter Eggs bring a new symbol of life, for example, for Christians it has been said that these Easter Eggs are used as a symbol for the resurrection of Jesus. Christians do believe that throughout his resurrection, overtime Jesus overcame his death and sins. Nowadays Easter eggs are made from chocolate which is covered with a colourful wrapper. As a tradition, chickens lay eggs which are then hard boiled and those will be decorate by hand, making them very colourful.

Everyone loves eating chocolate easter eggs, they can be really delicious but originally, they weren't allowed by the church leader during the week of Holy week and so any eggs that were laid during that week was to ensure that it was saved and decorated in order to make those Holy Week eggs, these were mainly given to children as gifts!

Easter eggs were mainly first appeared in France and Germany within the 19th Century considering they were bitter and hard, as chocolate making station have improved overtime, the hollow eggs have now been developed and has become very popular as well. The first hollow eggs were typically sold within the UK and was released in the year 1873 by a company that was called Fry's.
